# Physical AI Dataset Operations Console

> A Foundry/Workshop-style app for reviewing robotics data, deciding segment-level training usability, and turning QC evidence into operational action.

## What I Built

I built a portfolio version of a Foundry/Workshop-style Physical AI Dataset Operations Console to turn expensive robotics episodes into reviewable, decision-ready, and reusable training data units.

```text
Dashboard
→ Episode Review
→ Segment Review
→ Segment Decision
→ Training Span Impact
→ Follow-up Action / Export Readiness
```

## Why I Built It

Physical AI data is expensive, multimodal, and hard to reuse. The bottleneck was not simply collecting more data. The harder question was which parts of each episode were usable, what evidence supported that decision, who should review it, and what operational action should follow.

## How I Created It

I modeled the workflow around the decisions reviewers and operators actually need to make:

- Collection Episode
- Subtask Segment
- QC Check Result
- Segment Review
- Defect Attribution
- Action Recommendation
- Dataset Export Item

## Operational Value Created

- Reduces ambiguity in robotics data review.
- Makes partial salvage visible.
- Connects QC evidence to training usability decisions.
- Turns individual defects into operational recommendations.
- Clarifies when researcher escalation is needed.
- Helps raw episodes become reusable training-data assets rather than pass/fail leftovers.
