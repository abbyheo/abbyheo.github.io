Foundry Workshop app walkthrough · Physical AI data operations

Physical AI Data Review Console

A Palantir Foundry Workshop app that connects episode context, video/QC evidence, staged human review, defect attribution, and follow-up actions into a workflow for training handoff.

This page is a closer walkthrough of the Workshop app I rebuilt for my Physical AI portfolio case. The app is not a generic dashboard. It models how a team can move from messy Physical AI data collection results to review decisions, expert escalation, training-readiness judgment, and operational follow-up.

Scope note. The app uses representative demo data and a portfolio reconstruction of the workflow. QC signals support human review; they do not automatically decide training readiness. Segment windows are review cue windows for video navigation and action-context review, not claims of perfect hand-labeled action boundaries. The role-specific sections show staged review views and decision fields, not full production RBAC.

Why this app exists

Physical AI data review is not just a question of collecting more robot episodes. A collected episode may pass, fail, or be partially salvageable. Evidence can be scattered across video, sensor checks, raw packages, metadata, reviewer notes, and downstream export items. If the team only sees a flat pass/fail list, it cannot tell which segment is usable, why a case needs expert judgment, or what should change in future collection.

The app’s operating thesis is simple: collected episodes are not automatically training-ready data. The review system has to preserve the full collection context, localize issues to the relevant subtask/action context, record staged reviewer judgment, and connect the decision to dataset readiness and follow-up actions.

Workflow map

  1. Dashboard — batch state, expert escalation cases, all-episode review entry point, and yield-loss drivers.
  2. Episode Review Detail — selected episode summary, lineage, inherited session context, QC signals, same-episode segment decisions, and dataset package readiness.
  3. Segment Review Detail — selected segment video evidence, subtask criteria, decision evidence, Initial Reviewer context, Data PM context, Head Researcher closure, follow-up actions, and export readiness.
  4. Follow-up Action Detail — a recommended action with target, owner, priority, due date, status, source defect, affected segment, QC signal, and evidence traceability.

The repeated design choice is to keep each surface at the right grain: batch state at the dashboard, collection-attempt context at the episode level, training-use judgment at the segment level, and operational correction at the action level.

Dashboard: operating state before drilldown

The dashboard starts with operating state rather than raw tables. It tells the reviewer whether the current batch is moving toward training handoff, which segment cases required expert judgment, which episodes should be opened for deeper review, and which defect categories are reducing usable Physical AI data.

Head Researcher escalation cases

The Head Researcher lane is not a generic error list. It isolates segment-level cases that the Data PM could not safely close alone. Each row preserves the episode, selected segment, subtask goal, Data PM decision, escalation reason, escalation timestamp, final training decision, and review decision state.

That matters because the app protects expert time: the Head Researcher does not need to restart from zero or inspect every episode. They receive the cases where judgment is ambiguous or policy-sensitive, with the prior routing reason still attached.

All-episodes review table

The all-episodes table keeps decisions grounded in the original collection attempt. A reviewer can start from episode status, primary issue, source type, task, scenario, duration, and collection time before drilling into segment-level decisions. This prevents episode-level status from being confused with final segment-level training usability.

Episode Review Detail: full-run context before segment judgment

When a reviewer opens one episode, the app shows a full-run context page before asking the reviewer to make or inspect a segment decision. The selected episode summary separates the collection outcome from the number of usable segments. The lineage graph makes related records auditable. Inherited session context keeps collection method, robot platform, network profile, and data collector context attached to the episode.

A strong demo path is a partial-salvage real episode: the episode is not simply “good” or “bad,” because some segments can still be usable for training. That is the operational reason for drilling from episode context into selected segment review.

Segment Review Detail: evidence to staged training-use decision

Segment Review Detail is the central decision surface of the app. It focuses on one selected Subtask Segment and brings together the evidence, rubric, prior-stage decisions, final expert closure, follow-up actions, and dataset readiness around that single training-use unit.

What the reviewer sees

  • selected episode and segment context
  • review cue window for video navigation
  • subtask criteria and common failure modes
  • decision evidence: video, sensor, raw, and metadata context
  • Initial Reviewer and Data PM decisions with reasons
  • Head Researcher closure fields for final outcome, failure code, training decision, and span treatment

What the workflow changes

  • issues are localized to action context instead of vague episode-level comments
  • QC signals become review-routing evidence, not black-box decisions
  • expert review is reserved for cases that need expert judgment
  • segment-level decisions determine what can remain in the training path
  • review findings can continue into action recommendations and package readiness

The value is not that every boundary is perfectly hand-labeled. The value is that reviewers can ask a better operational question: which action context failed, what evidence supports that judgment, and which span should be excluded or preserved for training?

Evidence layer: video, QC, sensor, raw, and metadata context

The app keeps the selected segment decision grounded in evidence without turning the segment page into another episode-level QC table. A segment review can show multiple video views such as exo-top, wrist, or body camera views, plus sensor, raw, and metadata context. The evidence rows can also trace back to the QC signal and source defect when applicable.

This design keeps three things separate: the automated/rule-based QC signal, the evidence assets a reviewer inspects, and the human training-use decision. That separation is important because a warning can route a segment to review, but it should not replace reviewer judgment.

Closing the loop: review finding to follow-up action

The workflow does not stop once a segment is marked usable or not usable. If a defect is diagnosed, the Follow-up Action Detail page connects the defect to a concrete operational action: target, owner, priority, due date, status, source defect, affected segment, episode-level Check Result, and supporting evidence.

This is the customer-value layer of the app. A review finding should not remain a passive label. It should become a handoff: who owns the follow-up, what action should happen, when it is due, and which evidence record explains why it exists.
